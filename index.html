What You Need To Know

Some Information About Africa Music Week.
MUSIC CONNECTS THE WORLD

Get to Know More

As the cradle of rhythm, Africa’s rich arts, cultural and heritage history has over the years manifested herself in multiple platforms through music, dance and song. From North to South, East to West, African music and various artforms continue to immensely contribute to the growth and prosperity of the global music industry economy.

Yet Africa’s share of the global wealth generated from the creation and consumption of music content pales in comparison to her European, Asian and American counterparts. The situation has become even more dire now as the music industry economy globally migrates into the Forth Industrial Revolution (4IR) post Covid 19 driven by digital streaming where content is now king and the consumer queen.

To bridge the gap between content creation and its consumption with Africa getting an equitable share of the billions of U.S. dollars being generated from the annual global sale of content, African Music Week is on a mission to redress this trade deficit in favour of Africa.

African Music Week is embarking on a mission to empower each African country with the toolkit to setup its own ‘Music Week’ ecosystem as a destination to celebrate its arts, culture, heritage and to reclaim its share of the global music industry revenue. This will be done by each African country launching and hosting a 3-day annual African Music Week conference, festival, tech expo, B2B, industry awards, and publish a music industry market access export guide and directory. The event will become an opportunity for African countries to trade with each other, host continental and international music industry experts and practitioners from overseas countries while at the same time opening global export markets.

MUSIC CONNECTS THE WORLD

Our main Objectives

The primary aim and objectives of this concept document is to annually host a music conference and festival destination event in each African country to honour and celebrate African music, art and heritage by:

  • Developing the skills set and capacity of each African country to plan and host an African Music Week event
  • Rekindling Africa as the mecca of arts, culture and heritage in line with the principles of the African Union (AU) principles of promoting regional and continental economic integration
  • Creating skills development and employment opportunities in Africa and contribute towards social cohesion and economic development conflicts in Africa
  • Growing Africa Music into a world class festival with the potential to become a tourism destination of choice on the continent
  • Promoting Africa into a cohesive single export content market trading with each other
  • Position Africa as an economic and cultural gateway in Africa given its privileged geographical position, population size and economic might
  • Building a solid music industry infrastructure and trading corridor to compete globally
  • Showcasing the music and heritage of Africa to the world a leveraging its human capital wealth in mineral resources to advance a pan African development agenda and attract investment to the continent
  • Bridging the digital divide across the African continent
  • Promoting and intensifying regional trade, collaboration and tourism
